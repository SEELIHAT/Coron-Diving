About Decalachao River Wharf, Coron Palawan

Decalachao River Wharf is a tranquil riverside spot located in the northern part of Busuanga Island, Coron, Palawan. Nestled along the serene Decalachao River, this picturesque wharf serves as a gateway to adventure and relaxation. Stretching 6 kilometers before flowing into Maricaban Bay, it offers an idyllic setting for kayaking or stand-up paddling amidst lush mangrove trees and nipa palms. The wharf is not only a charming spot to unwind but also acts as a convenient pick-up and drop-off point for guests heading to Club Paradise Resort.

What Makes It Special

Decalachao River Wharf offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural significance. The surrounding mangrove forests provide a habitat for diverse wildlife, making it a haven for birdwatchers and nature enthusiasts. The calm waters of the river are perfect for kayaking and stand-up paddling, allowing visitors to immerse themselves in the tranquil environment. Additionally, the wharf serves as a gateway to Club Paradise Resort, offering a scenic sail through mangrove trees to the resort.

Highlights of Decalachao River Wharf

  • Scenic Views: Panoramic vistas of the Decalachao River and surrounding mangrove forests
  • Activities: Kayaking, stand-up paddling, bird watching, and nature photography
  • Accessibility: Convenient pick-up and drop-off point for Club Paradise Resort guests
  • Nearby Accommodations: The Riverhouse, a boutique resort offering tranquil lodging amidst lush greenery
