About the Course

The PADI Open Water Diver course is designed to introduce you to the basics of scuba diving. It is the world's most popular scuba diving course, and millions of people have started diving as certified PADI Open Water Divers.

The **PADI Open Water Diver Course** is your gateway to exploring the underwater world. As the most popular scuba diving certification worldwide, it opens the door to exciting adventures beneath the waves. Whether you’re new to diving or looking to build confidence in the water, this course provides the skills and knowledge you need to dive independently to depths of 18 meters (60 feet).

#### **What You’ll Learn** The course is designed to give you a solid foundation in diving safety, techniques, and equipment use. It typically consists of three main components: 1. **Knowledge Development** - Learn the principles of diving through online materials, classroom sessions, or independent study. - Topics include underwater communication, dive planning, buoyancy control, and the use of dive tables or computers. 2. **Confined Water Dives** - Practice essential skills in a controlled environment, such as a pool or shallow water. - Key skills include mask clearing, regulator recovery, and buoyancy techniques. 3. **Open Water Dives** - Put your training to the test in real-world diving scenarios under the guidance of a certified instructor. - Complete four dives to gain experience and confidence in open water.

What You Will Learn

  • Scuba diving equipment setup and usage
  • Underwater communication and safety procedures
  • Basic skills for diving in open water conditions
  • Planning and executing actual dives under the supervision of a PADI Instructor

Course Structure

The PADI Open Water Diver course consists of three main phases:

  1. Knowledge Development (Online or Classroom)
  2. Confined Water Dives to practice basic scuba skills
  3. Open Water Dives to review your skills and explore
