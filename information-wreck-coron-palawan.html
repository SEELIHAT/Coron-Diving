A Glimpse into History

During World War II, a fleet of Japanese supply ships was bombed and sunk by American forces in 1944. These wrecks now rest at various depths, creating an extraordinary diving experience. Over the years, they have transformed into artificial reefs, teeming with marine life and covered in stunning corals. Who Can Dive These Wrecks? Beginners can explore shallower wrecks like the Teru Kaze Maru and East Tangat Gunboat. Advanced divers can venture into deeper wrecks like the Okikawa Maru or the Akitsushima, a sunken seaplane tender with massive anti-aircraft guns still visible. Technical divers can go even further, exploring deep penetration areas and less-visited wrecks. Why Dive with Us? Our experienced PADI-certified instructors will guide you safely through these underwater historical sites, ensuring you get the best experience. Dive Sites in Coron, Palawan

Coron is a paradise for divers, offering a mix of world-class wreck dives, coral gardens, and vibrant marine life. Below is a list of the most popular dive sites in Coron, each with unique features and experiences:

  • 1. Irako Maru (Wreck)
    • Type: WWII Japanese Refrigeration Ship Depth: 28–40 meters Skill Level: Advanced Highlights: This upright wreck features excellent penetration opportunities and is rich in marine life, including groupers and trevallies. Visibility is typically good, showcasing its structure and coral growth.
  • 2. Akitsushima (Wreck)
    • Type: WWII Japanese Seaplane Tender Depth: 22–35 meters Skill Level: Advanced Highlights: Famous for its giant crane and massive propellers. Schools of batfish, barracudas, and colorful corals make this wreck a diver’s dream.
  • 3. Olympia Maru (Wreck)
    • Type: WWII Japanese Freighter Depth: 18–30 meters Skill Level: Beginner to Intermediate Highlights: A shallower wreck teeming with marine life, including snappers, lionfish, and anemones. The wreck is easy to navigate and a great introduction to wreck diving.
  • 4. Kogyo Maru (Wreck)
    • Type: WWII Japanese Freighter Depth: 22–34 meters Skill Level: Intermediate to Advanced Highlights: Divers can explore the ship’s holds, which still contain construction materials like cement bags and bulldozer parts.
  • 5. Morazan Maru (Wreck)
    • Type: WWII Japanese Freighter Depth: 12–25 meters Skill Level: Beginner to Intermediate Highlights: This wreck is upright and easy to penetrate, making it a favorite for those new to wreck diving. Its abundant marine life includes nudibranchs and large schools of fish.
  • 6. Kyokuzan Maru (Wreck)
    • Type: WWII Japanese Freighter Depth: 22–35 meters Skill Level: Advanced Highlights: Located farther from Coron town, this wreck is well-preserved, with trucks and motorcycles still visible in its cargo hold.
  • 7. Lusong Gunboat (Wreck)
    • Type: Shallow WWII Patrol Boat Depth: 3–15 meters Skill Level: Beginner Highlights: Perfect for snorkeling and shallow dives, the gunboat is a haven for coral growth and small fish.
  • 8. Barracuda Lake
    • Type: Fresh and Saltwater Lake Depth: 5–30 meters Skill Level: Beginner to Advanced Highlights: A unique dive with thermoclines and a mix of fresh and salt water. The lake is surrounded by limestone cliffs and is famous for its eerie, surreal atmosphere.
  • 9. Twin Peaks Reef
    • Type: Coral Reef Depth: 5–22 meters Skill Level: Beginner to Intermediate Highlights: Vibrant coral formations and abundant marine life, including clownfish, angelfish, and moray eels.
  • 10. Skeleton Wreck
    • Type: WWII Japanese Shipwreck Depth: 5–22 meters Skill Level: Beginner to Intermediate Highlights: A shallow wreck with fantastic coral growth. Ideal for snorkeling and introductory dives.
  • 11. East Tangat Wreck
    • Type: WWII Auxiliary Submarine Hunter Depth: 5–22 meters Skill Level: Beginner to Intermediate Highlights: A small, shallow wreck with colorful coral and an abundance of fish. Great for novice divers.
  • 12. Coral Garden
    • Type: Coral Reef Depth: 5–20 meters Skill Level: Beginner Highlights: A stunning reef teeming with healthy corals and marine life, including turtles and reef sharks.
  • 13. Calumbuyan Island Reef
    • Type: Coral Reef Depth: 3–20 meters Skill Level: Beginner Highlights: Crystal-clear waters and vibrant marine biodiversity make this reef a great spot for both diving and snorkeling.
  • 14. Black Island Wreck
    • Type: WWII Japanese Freighter Depth: 10–32 meters Skill Level: Advanced Highlights: A remote wreck dive with fewer crowds. The site features excellent coral coverage and diverse marine life. Why Dive in Coron? Coron offers a unique mix of history, adventure, and natural beauty. Whether you're interested in exploring sunken WWII ships or enjoying the colorful underwater life of its coral reefs, there’s a dive site for every level and preference.
